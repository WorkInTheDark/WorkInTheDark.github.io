Welcome to PhD 101

[Placeholder — replace with your first lesson, or leave this as the series opener.]

This is the first post in PhD 101, a series of short reflections I share with current and prospective PhD students. Each post is one lesson — something I either wish someone had told me earlier, or something I find myself telling my own students often enough that it is worth writing down.

A few things to know up front:

  • These are notes, not prescriptions. They reflect my own experience and biases. Other faculty will tell you the opposite of some of these and be just as right.
  • They will be short. If a lesson cannot fit in five minutes of reading, it probably needs to be two lessons.
  • They will evolve. I will revise posts as my own thinking changes. If you find something I said two years ago that no longer matches what I do today, the new version wins.

To add a new lesson, drop a markdown file into _phd101/ with frontmatter:

---
layout: page
title: Your Lesson Title
summary: One-sentence teaser.
order: 1
date: 2026-04-27
---

The landing page reads site.phd101 and sorts by order. Lower order appears first.
